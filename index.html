ICP Tiering Methodology

Configure how Panopto's ICP accounts will be scored and prioritized across Manufacturing and Non-Banking Finance.

How It Works
This workbook walks you through the scoring methodology we'll use to rank and prioritize ICP accounts across Manufacturing and Non-Banking Finance. You'll make decisions in six steps:
1. Target Accounts — Choose which accounts to include by filtering on size, revenue, and sub-industry.
2-4. Scoring Dimensions — Review how FIT (firmographics), SIGNALS (behavioral evidence), and TIMING (urgency indicators) contribute to each account's score.
5. Weights & Thresholds — Decide how much each dimension matters and where the Tier A/B/C cutoffs should be.
6. Review — See a complete summary and share your configuration.
Sensible defaults are already set — you can accept them or adjust anything. Your choices will be applied to score every account in the production run. The output is a tiered, prioritized list of accounts ready for outreach.
➕ Additive scoring: Signals found increase the score. Missing data is never penalized. Every account starts at zero and earns points based on detected evidence.

Step 2 of 6

FIT Parameters

Define how firmographic characteristics contribute to the FIT score.

🔄 You're now adjusting scoring, not filtering. From this point forward, all accounts remain in the pool. Your selections here determine how each account is scored and ranked — not whether it's included or excluded.
FIT measures how well a company matches Panopto's ideal customer profile based on firmographic characteristics. Companies that closely match these criteria may earn higher FIT scores.
Step 3 of 6

SIGNAL Parameters

Configure which behavioral signals are tracked and how much they influence the score.

🔄 Scoring mode: These settings affect how accounts are scored, not which accounts are included.
SIGNALS detect active indicators that a company may need or may already be investing in video-based learning and knowledge management. When these signals are found, it may increase confidence that the account is a strong prospect.
Step 4 of 6

TIMING Parameters

Configure how urgency and timing signals contribute to prioritization.

🔄 Scoring mode: These settings affect how accounts are scored, not which accounts are included.
Most accounts will have 1-2 verifiable timing signals. These indicators are detected by AI analysis of public sources — not all signals will be found for every account.
Step 5 of 6

Framework Weights & Thresholds

Now that you've seen what makes up each dimension, decide how much each should contribute to the final score.

🔄 Scoring mode: These settings affect how accounts are scored, not which accounts are included.
The Composite Score is calculated as: FIT × weight + SIGNALS × weight + TIMING × weight = Score (0-100). Adjust the weights to reflect what may matter most to Panopto's prioritization.
➕ Additive scoring: Signals found increase the score. Missing data is never penalized. Every account starts at zero and earns points based on detected evidence.
